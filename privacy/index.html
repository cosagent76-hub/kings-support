Privacy Policy

Effective July 15, 2026. This policy explains how Kings handles information when you use the iOS app.

Summary

  • Kings does not use advertising.
  • Kings does not use third-party analytics.
  • Kings does not track users across apps or websites.
  • Kings does not sell user data.
  • Player photos are intended to stay local on your device.
  • Optional scorebook sharing uses Apple's iCloud and CloudKit systems.

Information Stored by Kings

Kings stores scorekeeping information you enter into the app, such as player names, game results, hand scores, game history, and player stats. This information is used to provide the app's scorekeeping, history, and stats features.

If you add player photos, those photos are intended for the local player profile experience and are intended to stay on your device.

iCloud and CloudKit

Kings may use Apple's iCloud and CloudKit systems for optional family scorebook sharing. When you choose to use iCloud sharing, scorebook information may be stored in or shared through Apple's iCloud services under your Apple account and Apple's terms and privacy practices.

SCIM Ventures does not operate a separate account system or separate backend server for Kings.

Advertising, Analytics, and Tracking

Kings does not include advertising SDKs, third-party analytics SDKs, or third-party tracking SDKs. Kings does not track you across apps or websites owned by other companies.

Support Requests

If you request support through the public GitHub support form, the information you choose to include in that request may be publicly visible on GitHub. Do not include private personal information in public support requests.

Children's Privacy

Kings is a scorekeeper for a family card game and is not designed to collect personal information from children.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated when Kings changes. The effective date at the top of this page will be updated when the policy changes.

Contact

For support, visit the Kings Support page. Support requests opened through GitHub are public, so do not include sensitive personal information.
